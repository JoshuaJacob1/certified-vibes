The Process
Rigorous. Thorough.
Entirely Remote.

Our three-step certification process is designed to be as painless as possible — for everyone involved, especially you.

01
Submit Your Packaging

Upload a high-resolution image of your product label. Our expert panel will assess your color palette, typography, and whether any of your copy uses the word "pure," "natural," or "sun-kissed."

Farm photos optional. Stock images of meadows strongly encouraged.
02
Pass the Vibe Check

Within 3–5 business days, our certified Vibe Analysts will determine whether your branding communicates "sustainability" to the modern, environmentally-conscious consumer who doesn't read past the label.

Results not influenced by what actually happens on your farm.
03
Receive Your Seal

Display the Certified Vibes™ seal proudly on all packaging, marketing materials, and investor decks. What happens on your farm is between you, your soil, and no one at this organization.

Annual renewal required. Farming practices never re-reviewed.
Our Standards
What We Certify.
What We Don't.

Transparency is a core value here at Certified Vibes™. Here is exactly what our certification covers — and what falls, as we say, "outside our scope."

✓ Within Our Scope
✓
Label includes at least one shade of green Hex codes accepted. Pantone preferred.
✓
Copy features the words "natural," "wholesome," or "earth-friendly" Legal team has confirmed none of these are regulated terms.
✓
Packaging features an illustration of a farm, field, or sun Real farm not required. Illustrated barn strongly recommended.
✓
Font is earthy, hand-lettered, or "feels artisanal" Our analysts know it when they see it.
✓
Website includes the phrase "our commitment to the earth" No documentation of said commitment required.
✗ Outside Our Scope
✗
Soil health testing or carbon sequestration data We don't do science.
✗
Synthetic pesticide or fertilizer usage What you spray is your business.
✗
Whether tilling practices destroy carbon-storing topsoil Unnecessarily complicated. Outside our mandate.
✗
Cover cropping, composting loops, or water management Logistically challenging to verify. We've decided not to.
✗
Farm visits of any kind Rural roads are difficult. We're a remote-first organization.
